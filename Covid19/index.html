Frequently Asked Questions

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2.

How does COVID-19 spread?

COVID-19 spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. It can also spread by touching contaminated surfaces.

What are the common symptoms of COVID-19?

Common symptoms include fever, tiredness, dry cough, sore throat, aches and pains, and shortness of breath.

How can I protect myself from COVID-19?

You can protect yourself by wearing a face mask, washing your hands frequently, and avoiding close contact with sick people. Follow local health guidelines for more information.

What should I do if I suspect I have COVID-19?

If you have symptoms or have been in close contact with someone who tested positive, it's important to self-isolate, get tested, and follow local health guidelines for quarantine and treatment.

When will a COVID-19 vaccine be available?

COVID-19 vaccines are already available in many countries. Vaccination efforts are ongoing to provide immunity against the virus.
